LISA MOSES : : INTERDISCIPLINARY ARTIST + DESIGNER

lisa moses is an interdisciplinary artist and designer in denver, colorado. she currently teaches a variety of classes and develops curriculum at Rocky Mountain College of Art + Design. her freelance work is diverse, but she especially loves creating handmade and environmental graphics. when she is not in the studio, she is building low-income housing projects with Habitat for Humanity. the natural environment inspires her endlessly, as does nanotechnology, quantum theory and the structure of sound.

statement

For me, integrating multi-disciplinary beliefs and practices in my work is what makes possible deep, progressive engagement. As a Teacher, Artist and Designer, I promote this ethos and aspire to advance in a variety of disciplines.

Installation art was my preferred means of expression late in my undergraduate career. After graduating, I found myself digging deeper into issues concerning habitable spaces. I thought it apropos to study architecture. My ambitions were to understand the structure of built form and the consequences of formal composition, tectonic and spatial manipulation, and to more intimately appreciate and apprehend the experience of inhabitation in regards to structure, scale and site. I began to see my interests in art making shifting to a more macro level, as I wanted to define for myself what a confluence of art and architecture could be. I’m now developing these ideas further with a few processes: using ink drawings, handmade graphics, artist books, collages and paper works, I am exploring the human condition and our connection to the envrionment, and representing Gestalt perception of how we interpret our surroundings with repeated forms and patterning. Gestalt theory suggests we are driven to experience our immediate world in a holistic manner.

Along with these 2-dimensional explorations, I am also developing a large 3-dimensional experience with a musician. This collaboration will include an installation piece that will visually define the physics of sound traveling in space within a set of golden ratio tones. For me, sound is a profound medium across all cultures, especially music. Music is a universal and sometimes uniting form of art that can impact us at a very primal level. Understanding the holistic connections we makeas we hear music will be essential to constructing the structure. The final installation’s built forms will be defined by a performance of the golden ratio score.
